About

I’m a Mechanical Engineering graduate student at Carnegie Mellon University with a 4.0 GPA, building on a strong academic foundation from Penn State University. My current research focuses on mobile manipulators and computer vision, where I work on integrating real-time feedback into robotic planning and control. I’m especially interested in closing the gap between theoretical algorithms and practical robotic systems—making machines not only smarter on paper but also more reliable in the real world.

I enjoy tackling problems that sit at the intersection of mechanics, perception, and control, and I like to think of myself as an engineer who can translate math-heavy concepts into systems that actually do something useful (and hopefully don’t catch fire in the process).

Research and Projects

Vision-Based Mobile Manipulator Control

Carnegie Mellon University • 2024-Present
Watch Demo

Developed and integrated a real-time computer vision-based feedback module for mobile manipulators, achieving exceptional precision with mean deviation of 0.75 mm and RMS error of 0.92 mm across 72,934 sampled points. The system enables manipulators to adapt dynamically in complex environments through advanced point cloud processing and real-time control algorithms.

Dual Harvesting Radiative Cooling Device

Penn State University • 2022-2024 • Published in Cell Reports Physical Science
Read Paper

Co-developed a dual-function device combining radiative cooling with solar energy generation, achieving sub-ambient cooling up to 4.9°C and >8.8 W/m² solar output. Built and aligned optical/thermal components for optimal performance, validated through comprehensive testing showing ~55 W/m² net cooling at night.

RF Test Fixture Cost Optimization

Bojay Technologies • 2025

Led cost optimization efforts for custom RF test fixtures serving major tech clients including Meta, Apple, Tesla, and NVIDIA. Evaluated RF-compatible materials and redesigned fixtures, achieving over 66% reduction in material costs while maintaining performance standards. Managed cross-functional collaboration between ME/EE teams and client communications.

Colored Radiative Cooling Paint

Penn State REU Program • 2023

Proposed and developed colored paint formulations with controlled polymer pore sizes for thermal performance. Fabricated paint samples in wet lab environment and conducted comprehensive analysis using UV-Vis-NIR spectrophotometry. Successfully created paint samples with measurable cooling efficiency while maintaining aesthetic color options for architectural applications.
